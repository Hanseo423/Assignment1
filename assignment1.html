King Sejong the Great

Biography

King Sejong (1397–1450), the fourth king of the Joseon Dynasty in Korea, is best known for creating Hangul, the Korean alphabet, in 1443.
At the time, most people struggled with the complex Chinese characters used in writing,
so King Sejong sought to develop a simpler, phonetic script that everyone could easily learn and use.
His invention of Hangul, originally called Hunminjeongeum, revolutionized literacy in Korea,
making written communication accessible to all, regardless of social class, and remains one of his greatest legacies.
